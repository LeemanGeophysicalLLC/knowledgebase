## Overview
The tissue disruptor is an instrument intended to make processing organic samples for DNA extraction easier and faster.  The tissue disruptor is designed to work with the Axygen™ Mini Tube System (MTS118C) and 5/32” ball bearings, though is versatile enough to accommodate many other sample holding systems. It is designed to connect to a standard reciprocating saw using the blade adapter, making processing as easy as pulling the trigger.

This design was adapted from a design by Dr. James Beck who designed his disruptor based upon the following manuscript. We thank Dr. Beck for sharing his prototype and encouraging the production of this unit.

<small>
Alexander, Patrick J., et al. "Recovery of plant DNA using a reciprocating saw and silica‐based columns." Molecular Ecology Notes 7.1 (2007): 5-9.
</small>

### Hazards/PPE
<B>CAUTION!</B> Always use proper PPE when dealing with power tools, liquid nitrogen, and organic samples. Consult your environmental health and safety representative for recommendations and requirements. Always ensure all screws are secure and that the area is clear before operating this or any other power tool.

## Operation
The tissue disruptor ships pre-assembled, but some setup is still required.

<ol>
    <li>Inspect all screws connecting the blade adapter to the top plate and ensure they are tight. Vibration could cause them to become loose. Tighten if necessary.
    </li>
    <li>Place the samples into the mini tubes along with two 5/32” stainless steel ball bearings.
    </li> 
    <li>Insert the sample tubes into the instrument so that it is held tightly between the two plates. Tighten the clamps using the hand knobs.
    </li>
    <li>Attach the instrument to a reciprocating saw using the blade adapter as shown.
        <div style="text-align: center;">
        <img src="../action_photo.png" alt="Instrument cover photo" style="height:300px;">
        </div>
    </li>
    <li>Submerge the instrument in liquid nitrogen for optimal results.
    </li>
    <li>Hold the reciprocating saw and pull the trigger to begin processing the samples, continue until the desired consistency is achieved.
    </li>
</ol>
